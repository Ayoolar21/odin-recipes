Egusi soup and Paundo yam recipe

This egusi soup and paundo yam recipe is for how to make egusi soup that is delicious and quick, in easy steps.

Truth is there are many methods of cooking egusi soup. There is Egusi Ijebu, there's lumpy egusi for those who likes egusi balls and here I am giving you another method of cooking Egusi soup.

Ingredients

  • 2 Cups melon seed
  • 3 maggi cubes (knorr maggi or any stock cube of your choice)
  • 1 cup palm oil
  • 3 scotch bonnet(ata rodo)
  • 2 bell pepper(big tatashe)
  • 1 red onion
  • Assorted meat
  • Dry shawa
  • smoked dry cat fish
  • Freshly prepared snails
  • 1/4 cup grounded cray fish
  • locust beans
  • Beef stock
  • 100g pumpkin leaves or Kale(Ugwu)
  • Salt to taste
  • and Ayoola Paundo Powder

Steps in preparing the meal

  1. Wash, season and boil the beef or chicken and assorted meats.
  2. Blend the scotch bonnet, bell pepper, plum tomato and onion.
  3. Blend the brown onion with the melon seeds to form a thick paste.
  4. Clean the smoked dry fish by soaking in hot water for few minutes and rinsing well
  5. n a pot, add palm oil, leave to heat for 2 minutes then add the blended pepper, maggi cubes and beef stock. Cover the pot and leave to cook for 10 minutes.
  6. Add smoked fish, beef, assorted meat, locust beans and crayfish. Stir together and leave to cook for another 10 minutes.
  7. After the 10 minutes, open the pot and stir the stew. Now add the dry fish, assorted meat and locust at this stage and let it cook for another 10 minutes.
  8. Scoop in the onion/egusi paste, cover the pot and leave to cook for yet another 10 minutes
  9. Stir the soup and leave to cook for 5 minutes or till oil floats to the top.
  10. At the end of the 5 minutes, stir and add vegetable to the soup.
  11. Then add Kale or Ugu
  12. Stir in the vegetable and leave to simmer for 5 minutes.

Finally, you have Ofe egusi
